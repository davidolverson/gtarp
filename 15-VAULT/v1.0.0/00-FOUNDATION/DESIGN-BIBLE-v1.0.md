## 1. Mission
Palm6 exists to deliver a premium, cinematic roleplay experience that feels like its own complete game - not "another GTA server."

We build with the same discipline, consistency, and polish that Rockstar applies to its own brands. Players should feel they are stepping into a living, breathing world with its own identity, history, economy, and visual language.

## 2. Vision
When Palm6 launches, people won't say "That's a really good FiveM server."

They will say:  
**"How did they make this look and feel like its own game?"**

We achieve this through obsessive consistency, professional-grade design systems, and world-building that rewards long-term investment in character, business, and reputation.

## 2.5 Design Philosophy (North Star)
Palm6 is built like a premium fictional universe with real-world production values.

We reject "good enough for RP" aesthetics. Every element - from a police badge to a luxury real estate logo to a phone notification - must feel intentional, cohesive, and timeless. 

The goal is not to imitate Rockstar. The goal is to earn the same level of respect for craft, attention to detail, and world integrity that players associate with the best single-player titles. 

This means:
- Restraint over excess
- Clarity over decoration
- Consistency over novelty
- Long-term thinking over short-term trends

If an asset feels like it could exist in a high-budget game or a sophisticated brand campaign, we're on the right track.

## 3. Core Principles & Permanent Rules

**Rule 1: No AI-looking fonts or generic aesthetics.**  
Everything uses professional, clean, commercial-quality typography. No cheap gradients, overused esports effects, random shields, wings, crowns, or lions just because it's RP.

**Rule 2: Everything belongs to one universe.**  
A bank logo, police patch, nightclub flyer, government website, and vehicle livery must feel like they were designed by the same creative studio in the same year.

**Rule 3: Quality beats quantity.**  
We would rather ship one exceptional, timeless asset than ten average ones. Every deliverable must pass the Design Review Checklist (see section 10).

**Rule 4: Player identity is deeper than jobs.**  
Characters build real reputation through business success, criminal notoriety, driving skill, property ownership, social influence, and achievements. The systems we create must support long-term character progression and status.

### Player Identity as a Core Design Pillar
This is one of Palm6’s key differentiators from typical RP servers.

We design systems and visuals that make reputation, status, and personal history *visible* and *meaningful*:
- Business prestige and corporate branding that players can build and wear.
- Criminal notoriety that affects how law enforcement and civilians interact with them.
- Driving licenses, property portfolios, and social standing that evolve over time.
- Achievement and faction reputation that influence opportunities and aesthetics (e.g., high-end vs. street-level customization options).

Every major system (phone, MDT, business panels, vehicle liveries, clothing, events) should reinforce the feeling that your character’s journey matters and is reflected in the world.

## 4. Brand Personality
- **Confident but not arrogant**
- **Polished and cinematic**
- **Tropical luxury with grit** (Palm6 State has beautiful coastlines, wealthy districts, industrial ports, and real crime/problems)
- **Modern, clean, and premium** - think high-end real estate branding meets grounded law enforcement and street-level authenticity
- **Warm yet professional** - inviting for new players, respected by veterans

**Tone of Voice (for all official communication):**
- Direct, clear, and slightly elevated.
- Never overly casual or meme-heavy in official channels.
- Marketing can be energetic; government and corporate materials are authoritative and clean.

## 5. Visual Identity System

### Typography
We use a small, carefully chosen set of high-quality typefaces across all touchpoints.

**Display / Headlines** (logos, titles, large signage):
- Primary: Monument Extended or Neue Haas Grotesk Display (strong, modern, architectural)
- Secondary / Motorsport feel: Eurostile Extended (for vehicles, racing, industrial)

**Body / UI Text:**
- Inter or Geist (excellent readability, modern, versatile)
- General Sans as backup

**Numbers & Data (MDT, CAD, dashboards, pricing):**
- DIN or Eurostile (technical, clear, authoritative)

**Rules:**
- Never use decorative or "AI-generated" script/serif fonts for primary branding.
- Strict hierarchy: One display face + one body face is usually enough.
- All text must remain highly legible at small sizes (mobile, in-game UI, distance signage).

### Color System (Initial Direction - to be refined with actual palette)
Palm6 draws from tropical luxury, coastal Florida grit, and modern corporate aesthetics.

**Core Direction:**
- Deep ocean blues and teals
- Warm sunset golds and corals
- Rich forest greens and earth tones for natural areas
- Clean neutrals (charcoal, warm off-white, soft black) for UI and corporate
- Strategic use of high-contrast accents for police/government (navy + gold) vs. luxury (gold + deep teal)

**Usage Rules:**
- Primary brand color must work in both light and dark environments.
- Government and emergency services use more restrained, authoritative palettes.
- Luxury and entertainment districts can use richer, warmer tones.
- Never use more than 3–4 colors in any single asset unless it's a deliberate gradient or illustration.

(Exact hex values, tints, shades, and accessibility contrast rules will be locked in COLOR-SYSTEM.md after palette finalization.)

### Motion & Animation Principles
- Smooth, purposeful, cinematic.
- Subtle micro-interactions in UI (gentle spring, clean fades).
- Logo reveals and loading sequences should feel premium and intentional.
- Avoid overly bouncy or "gamey" animations unless contextually appropriate (e.g., arcade-style in certain entertainment venues).
- Performance is non-negotiable - animations must not impact gameplay.

### Iconography & UI Language
- Clean, geometric, consistent stroke weight.
- Rounded corners on cards and buttons (modern, friendly but professional).
- Strong use of negative space.
- Icons must work at 24px and scale beautifully.
- Glassmorphism or subtle depth only where it enhances readability and premium feel - never as a crutch.

## 6. World-Building Rules
- Palm6 is its own fictional state (not Leonida or any existing GTA map).
- Every district, business, government agency, and landmark must have coherent history, economy, and visual identity.
- Signage, architecture, and environmental storytelling must match the brand language.
- The world supports both high-society luxury and grounded street-level RP.

## 7. UI / Digital Product Language
One unified operating system across:
- Phone
- Inventory
- Garage
- Banking
- MDT / CAD
- Business management panels
- Housing
- Dispatch

All interfaces share the same typography, color rules, button styles, spacing, icon set, and animation language. The phone OS especially must feel like a premium, modern device that belongs in this world.

## 8. Marketing & Social Standards
- Every public-facing asset must pass the Design Review Checklist.
- Social content calendar respects the brand personality.
- Creator program assets (overlays, alerts, thumbnails) must feel like official Palm6 extensions, not generic streamer kits.
- Trailers and launch materials aim for cinematic quality.

## 9. Internal Development Standards
Every major feature or asset goes through:
1. Design spec (reference to this Bible)
2. UX flow
3. Acceptance criteria
4. QA checklist (including visual consistency)
5. Accessibility & performance review
6. Documentation update

This discipline is how we maintain quality as the project scales.

## 10. Design Review Checklist (Mandatory)
Before anything is considered complete, ask:

- Does it match the current DESIGN-BIBLE (typography, colors, motion, personality)?
- Is it readable and effective at all intended sizes and distances?
- Does it feel like it belongs in the Palm6 universe alongside other approved assets?
- Would this still look good and relevant in 12–18 months?
- Is the quality at a level where someone could reasonably mistake it for official Rockstar-level work?
- Does it support (or at least not contradict) deeper player identity and long-term progression systems?

If the answer to any question is no or uncertain, revise.
