## 1. Design Direction
Palm6 draws from **tropical luxury with grounded grit**:
- Coastal Florida elegance meets modern corporate authority.
- Deep ocean tones for trust and depth.
- Warm metallics for premium feel.
- Strategic coral and green accents for energy and nature.
- Restrained neutrals for UI and longevity.

The palette must work across digital (UI, web, social), print, in-game signage, vehicle liveries, and physical merch.

## 3. Usage Rules (Mandatory)

**Primary Brand Application**
- Deep Ocean Teal is the dominant color for most logos, wordmarks, and primary buttons.
- Warm Heritage Gold is used for premium accents and secondary logos.
- Never use Coral as the main brand color — it is an accent only.

**Government & Emergency Services**
- Use Navy Authority as the primary color.
- Deep Ocean Teal and Gold as secondary.
- High contrast, authoritative feel. Avoid warm coral in official government materials.

**Luxury / Entertainment / Lifestyle**
- Can lean more heavily into Warm Heritage Gold + Coral accents.
- Still anchored by Deep Ocean Teal for cohesion.

**UI & Digital Products**
- Backgrounds: Warm Off-White (light) or Charcoal/Soft Black (dark).
- Text: Charcoal or Soft Black on light; Warm Off-White on dark.
- Primary actions: Deep Ocean Teal.
- Accents: Warm Heritage Gold.
- Maintain minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for all text (WCAG AA).

**Environmental & In-Game**
- Signage and architecture should feel cohesive with the above.
- Natural areas favor Forest Ground + Deep Ocean Teal.
- Industrial/port areas can use more Charcoal + Navy.

**Never**
- Use more than one accent color prominently in a single asset unless it's a deliberate multi-color illustration or map.
- Use bright "FiveM neon" colors or generic esports gradients.

## 4. Tints, Shades & Variations
- Create tints (lighter) and shades (darker) of each core color for hover states, backgrounds, and hierarchy.
- Example for Deep Ocean Teal:
  - 80% tint: #3A7A8F
  - 60% tint: #6BA7B9
  - 40% shade: #073A4F
- All tints/shades must maintain brand recognition.

## 5. Accessibility & Performance
- All text on colored backgrounds must meet WCAG AA (4.5:1) or AAA (7:1) where possible.
- Test palettes in both light and dark environments.
- Colors must reproduce well in print (CMYK conversion) and on various screens.
- Avoid relying on color alone to convey meaning (use icons + text).
