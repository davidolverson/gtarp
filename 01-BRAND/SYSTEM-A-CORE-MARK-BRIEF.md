## Why this brief exists

CD-001 is the last open item on the brand half of the Phase 2 quality gate. The
24 department crests and 2 Verano state seals are placed and registered, but those
are System B marketing art and formal seals, a different asset class. The one thing
Palm6 still does not have is its **System A core identity mark**: the primary,
ownable, timeless logo that represents Palm6 itself. Until it exists, the "System A
reads in one color at 32px" gate cannot be verified (this is exactly the single open
item DEC-004 flagged). This brief locks the direction so David can generate the mark
in ChatGPT and bring it back for review.

## What the System A core mark IS

- The **primary, official, timeless representation of Palm6**. It is the wordmark
  and/or the P6 monogram that goes on the favicon, official documents, the hero
  crest, merchandise (embroidery, engraving), and anywhere Palm6 signs its own name.
- **System A (Identity)**, per `05-DUAL-VISUAL-SYSTEM.md`: simple, minimal, luxury,
  timeless, scalable, and correct in one flat color. The reference bar is Porsche,
  Rolex, Apple, Rimowa, marks that are strong enough to stand alone with no effects.
- **City-level.** Verano is the state, Palm6 is the city (see the Verano state seals
  row in the registry). This mark is the **Palm6 city identity**, not a Verano state
  seal. It should read as a peer of, and be visually distinct from, the formal
  circular Verano seals, never a smaller copy of them.
- Designed in **pure black first**. The brand-color applications (Deep Ocean Teal
  `#0A4D68`, Warm Heritage Gold `#B8860B` per `COLOR-SYSTEM.md`) are downstream
  variations of a mark that must already be perfect in one flat color.

## What the System A core mark IS NOT

- **Not** a System B cinematic/marketing treatment. No atmosphere, no lighting, no
  "GTA6 feel." That emotion lives in System B key art, not in the logo.
- **Not** a business sub-logo. This is not one of the 14 private-business marks
  (Apex Motors, Bayside Realty, and the rest) and it is not built on their lockup or
  palette. It sits above them as the parent city brand.
- **Not** a department crest or a Verano state seal. Those already exist and are a
  different asset class.
- **Not** trend-chasing. No synthwave, no neon, no palm-tree-sunset scene (the exact
  cliche that got the first private-business batch rejected).

## Acceptance checklist (the binding gate)

The mark is not Approved unless every line is a clear yes. These restate
`07-QUALITY-STANDARDS.md` and the checklist in `08-DESIGN-REVIEW-CHECKLIST.md`.

- [ ] **32px legible.** Recognizable and readable at 32px (favicon scale). Test it,
      do not assume it.
- [ ] **One flat color.** Works as solid black on white (and reversed white on
      black) with no loss of meaning. No gradients, no chrome, no glow, no bevel,
      no drop shadow, no inner shadow.
- [ ] **No photographic or 3D effects.** Flat vector construction only. It must not
      rely on texture, lighting, or rendering to look good.
- [ ] **Timeless (5+ year horizon).** Would still look right in five years. No
      short-term style that dates it.
- [ ] **Reproducible by hand and at any size.** Someone could redraw it, embroider
      it, engrave it, or laser-cut it. Construction is simple enough to survive that.
- [ ] **Ownable and original.** Distinct enough to be defensibly Palm6, not a stock
      shape or a near-copy of an existing brand.
- [ ] **Distinct from the 14 business logos and the Verano seals.** Reads as the
      parent city brand, not a member of either set.
- [ ] **Strong negative space.** Generous, balanced clear space; nothing crowded.
- [ ] **Strengthens the Palm6 universe.** Feels like it belongs to the same premium
      world as the rest of the system.

If any answer is unclear or no, it stays Candidate and goes back for refinement.

## Required deliverable set from David

Bring back all of the following so the mark can be reviewed and registered as a
complete System A asset, not a single loose image:

1. **Primary mark.** The main lockup (wordmark, or monogram plus wordmark) at full
   quality, on transparent background.
2. **One-color master.** Pure black (`#000000`) on transparent. This is the master
   from which all colored versions derive.
3. **Reversed version.** Pure white on transparent, for dark backgrounds.
4. **Minimum-size / 32px test.** The mark rendered at 32px so legibility can be
   verified directly against the gate, not inferred.
5. **Clear-space note.** A short statement of the minimum clear space around the
   mark (for example, "clear space equals the height of the P6 counter on all
   sides") plus the minimum print/screen size below which it must not be used.

Optional but welcome: a standalone monogram (P6 icon) if the wordmark and monogram
are separable, since the favicon and merch often need the icon alone.

## Copy-paste ChatGPT prompt variants

Each variant bakes in the one-color, 32px, flat-vector, no-effects, no-slop
constraints. Generate a few, pick the strongest direction, then refine that one.
Ask ChatGPT for the **pure-black-on-white version first**; only add color once the
black master is right.

### Variant 1: Wordmark-led (editorial luxury)

> Design a **flat vector wordmark logo** for **"Palm6"**, the primary identity mark
> of a premium, modern coastal city brand. Style: minimal, timeless, luxury, in the
> spirit of Porsche, Rolex, and Apple wordmarks. Use a **confident custom or refined
> geometric/grotesk letterform**, tight and intentional spacing, strong negative
> space. **Pure solid black on a white background. Flat vector only: no gradient, no
> chrome, no metallic, no 3D bevel, no drop shadow, no glow, no texture, no
> lighting.** It must stay **fully legible and recognizable at 32px** (favicon
> scale) and reproduce cleanly in a single flat color for embroidery and engraving.
> **No palm-tree-sunset scene, no neon, no synthwave.** Centered, generous margins,
> transparent-ready. Timeless enough to look right in five years.

### Variant 2: P6 monogram-led (mark plus wordmark)

> Design a **flat vector monogram logo** built from the characters **"P6"** for
> **Palm6**, a premium coastal city brand. One **simple, ownable geometric monogram**
> that reads clearly as P6, optionally locked above or beside a **clean minimal
> "Palm6" wordmark** in one consistent sans-serif. Style: minimal, luxury, timeless
> (Rimowa / Apple restraint). **Solid black on white, flat vector only: no gradient,
> no chrome, no 3D, no bevel, no drop shadow, no glow, no texture.** Must be
> **recognizable at 32px** as a favicon and reproducible by hand at any size, with
> **strong balanced negative space**. It must look like a **parent city brand**,
> distinct from ordinary business logos and from formal circular government seals.
> **No palm-sunset cliche, no neon gradients, no esports look.** Centered,
> transparent background, even padding.

### Variant 3: Single ownable symbol plus wordmark (restrained motif)

> Design a **flat vector logo** for **Palm6**, a premium modern coastal city, made of
> **one simple ownable symbol** (a single restrained motif such as a minimal palm
> mark abstracted to a geometric glyph, or a "6" integrated into a clean architectural
> or horizon shape) paired with a **minimal "Palm6" wordmark** in one confident
> sans-serif. Keep the symbol **abstract and iconic, not a detailed illustration or a
> literal beach scene**. Style: minimal, timeless, luxury. **Pure black on white,
> flat vector only: no gradient, no chrome, no metallic, no 3D bevel, no drop shadow,
> no glow, no photographic or lighting effects.** Must survive at **32px** in one flat
> color and be **redrawable by hand**. **Absolutely no palm-tree-sunset scene, no
> neon, no rainbow gradient, no drop shadows.** Strong negative space, centered,
> transparent background.

**Keep the constraint words identical across every regeneration.** Only vary the
concept (wordmark vs monogram vs symbol). The constraints are what keep the output
inside System A.

## Where the output lives and how it gets Approved

- **Location:** the delivered files go in `01-BRAND/logos/` (a `core/` subfolder is
  fine, alongside `departments/` and `state/`), with a short README noting the
  variant chosen, the clear-space rule, and the minimum size.
- **Status on delivery: Candidate.** It registers as Candidate in
  `17-ASSET-REGISTRY/ASSET-REGISTRY.md` (advancing the existing "Palm6 System A core
  identity mark" row from Experimental to Candidate).
- **Promotion to Approved** requires passing `08-DESIGN-REVIEW-CHECKLIST.md` against
  the acceptance checklist above **and** a new `00-FOUNDATION/09-DECISION-LOG.md`
  entry whose ID is written into the registry's Decision Log Ref column. No System A
  asset is Approved before that entry exists (this is the CD-001 close condition).
- On Approved, fill the System A specifics in `01-BRAND/BRAND-GUIDELINES.md` and copy
  the master into `15-VAULT/`.
